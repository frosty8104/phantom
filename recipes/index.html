Grain Free German Oven Pancakes

(Source: Fast Paleo)

  • 4 large eggs, yolks and whites separated
  • 1/4 cup non-dairy milk
  • 1/4 cup lukewarm water
  • /1/2 cup arrowroot powder
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract (optional)
  • 1/8 tsp sea salt
  • 3 tbsp butter or coconut oil

Preheat oven to 400F. Combine 4 yolks and salt and whisk until thick. Whisk in milk, water, and vanilla. Add in arrowroot and whisk until smooth. In a separate bowl, beat egg whites until stiff, but not dry, peaks form. Add the whites to the yolk mixture, gently whisk until the whites are incorporated, but not deflated. Light foam is what you want. Melt 3 tbsp butter in a 10 inch oven safe skillet over medium heat. Once the bubbles have subsided, pour the batter into the skillet and cook until the bottom is golden (about 2 minutes). Place the skillet in the oven and bake until it puffs up and the top is golden and feels dry and set (about 10-12 minutes). Slide onto a plate and serve with hot butter and syrup, fresh fruit sauce or warm preserves.

Paleo Chicken Stuffing

(Source: paleofood.com)

  • 2 cups finely ground blanched almonds (or almond meal)
  • 1 cup chopped onion (use dried chopped onion for better flavour)
  • 1/2 cup chopped celery
  • 1 tsp ground sage
  • 1 tsp ground thyme
  • chopped parsley
  • 1 Tbsp mild-flavoured oil (more if it seems too dry)
  • pepper to taste

Mix together all ingredients. Fill cavity of bird with mixture, then roast.

Note: I've found I prefer the stuffing cooked in a separate dish instead of in the bird. Also, we like to spice things up at my family's house, so we add, at the very least, cranberries and mushrooms to it.

Slow Cooker Butter Chicken

  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 4 large skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sizepieces
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp curry powder
  • 1 Tbsp curry paste
  • 1 tsp tandoori masala
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 (6 ounce) can tomato paste
  • 15 green cardamon pods
  • 1 (14 ounce) can coconut milk
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • salt to taste

Melt the butter and vegatable oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Stir in the chicken, onion, and garlic. Cook and stir until the onion has softened and turned translucent, about 10 minutes. Stir in the curry powder, curry paste, tandoori masala, garam masala, and tomato paste until no lumps of tomato paste remain. Pour into a slow cooker, and stir in the cardamon pods, coconut milk, and yogurt. Season to taste with salt.

Cook on high 4 - 6 hours, or on low 6 - 8 hours until the chicken is tender and the sauce has reduced to your desired consistency. Remove and discard the cardamon pods before serving. 6 servings

Paleo Pie Crust

(Source: Wholesome Yum)

  • 2 - 1/2 C almond flour
  • 1/4 tsp sea salt (1/2 tsp if savoury)
  • 1/4 C ghee (measured solid, then melted)
  • 1 large egg (or 2 Tbsp ghee)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract (optional)

1. Preheat oven to 350 F. Line the bottom of a 9" pan with parchment paper, or grease well.

2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the almond flour and salt.

3. Stir in the melted ghee and egg until well combined. (If using vanilla, stir that into the ghee before adding dry ingredients.) The "dough" will be dry and crumbly. Just keep mixing, pressing, and stirring until it's uniform, and there's no almond flour powder left. (Alternatively, you can use a food processor to mix it all together.)

4. Press the dough into the bottom of the prepared pan. You can flute the edges if desired; if it crumbles while doing this, just press it back together. Carefully poke holes in the surface using a fork to prevent bubbling.

5. Bake for 10 - 12 minutes until lightly golden brown. (Add fillings only after pre-baking.)

Paleo Pizza Crust

(Source: Paleo Cupboard)

  • 2 large eggs, room temperature (variation: chia 'egg' - 1 Tbsp ground chia, 3 Tbsp water)
  • 2 Tbsp olive oil
  • 1 - 1/2 almond flour
  • 1/4 C tapioca flour
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp basil
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder

1. Place the eggs and oil in a small mixing bowl and stir until well combined. Set aside.

2. Combine the rest of the ingredients in a medium size mixing bowl and stir with a fork to break apart clumps and to combine well. Add the egg mixture to the flour mixture and stir well until combined. Set aside for about 5 - 10 minutes and then split in half and form into two round balls.

3. Preheat oven to 375F. Bake crust for 15 minutes or until firm.

"No Rolls" Dinner Rolls

Gluten-Free | Dairy Free | Egg Free | Vegan | High Fiber | Moderate-Carb

    Ingredients
  • Dry Ingredients
    • 3/4 cup almond flour (75g)
    • 1/2 cup arrowroot flour (60g)
    • 1/4 cup tapioca flour (25g)
    • 1/4 cup coconut flour (40g)
    • 3/4 tsp. sea salt
    • 1 tsp. non-aluminum baking powder
    • 1 Tbsp. active dry yeast
    • 1 Tbsp. organic psyllium husk powder (12g)
  • Wet Ingredients
    • 1 tsp. raw honey (or maple syrup)
    • 1 cup filtered water
    • 1 Tbsp. palm shortening, melted
    • Egg wash (1 egg yolk + 1 tsp water) - optional
      Directions
    • In a large bowl, whisk together almond flour, arrowroot flour, tapioca flour, coconut flour, baking powder, and sea salt.
    • Heat water to 105 - 110 degrees F. Add honey or maple syrup and stir. Add the yeast and let it stand 10 minutes. The yeast should bubble or foam - if it doesn't - start over.
    • Whisk the psyllium powder into the yeast mixture to fully combine (no lumps!). Let stand 1 minute to thicken. Whisk in the melted shortening.
    • Pour the thickened yeast-psyllium mixture into the dry ingredients and mix well with a wooden spoon or silicone spatula to form a sticky dough.
    • Cover with a damp kitchen towel and place in a warm spot to rise for one hour or until doubled in size.
    • Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Prepare a baking sheet with parchment or by lightly greasing.
    • Punch dough down and knead for one minute. Divide dough into 8 balls and place on prepared pan. Using wet hands, smooth the top of the dough. You may use a serrated knife to cut an "X" or tic-tac-toe pattern on the top to allow fot the bread to expand. Brush with egg wash, if using. Place in a warm spot to let rise for another 30 minutes until the oven preheats.
    • Transfer to oven and bake for 30 minutes or until internal temperature reaches 200-210 F.
    • Let cool completely on a wire rack. (Note: not fully colling results in a gummy texture. Let the moisture redistribute and dissapate) When completely cool, store in an airtight container

Crusty Boule

Gluten-Free | Dairy Free | Egg Free | Vegan | High Fiber | Moderate-Carb

    Ingredients
  • Dry Ingredients
    • 1 cup almond flour (100 g)
    • 1 cup arrowroot (120 g)
    • 1/3 cup coconut flour (40 g)
    • 1 tsp. salt
    • 2 tsp. active dry yeast
    • 1 1/2 Tbsp. very finely ground chia (18 g)
    • 2 Tbsp. organic psyllium husk powder (24 g)
  • Wet Ingredients
    • 1 1/4 cup filtered water
    • 2 tsp. maple sugar (or coconut sugar or honey)
    • 1 egg + 1 tsp. water for egg wash (optional)
    • In a large bowl, whisk together almond flour, arrowroot flour, coconut flour, and sea salt.
    • Heat water to 105 - 110 degrees F. Add 2 tsp. maple syrup and stir. Add the yeast ad let stand 10 minutes. The yeast should bubble or foam - if it doesn't - start over. Stir the finely ground chia and psyllium powder into yeast mixture. Let stand 1 minute to thicken, then whisk.
    • Pour the thickened yeast-chia mixture into the dry ingredients and mix with a wooden spoon until thick and fully combined. The dough will be slightly sticky, but workable. Knead for 1 minute.
    • Put kneaded dough back in the bowl, cover with damp kitchen towel and place in a warm spot to rise for an hour.
    • Prehead the oven to 425 degrees F. Place a pizza stone in the oven to preheat.
    • Divide dough into two balls. Place dough balls in a square of parchment or a pizza peel. Dip your hands in water and shape into nice rounded shapes (it will rise in the oven, so you may want to flatten the loaves a bit). Brush with egg wash, if using. Use a serrated knife to cut a tic-tac-toe pattern on the top.
    • Slide the prepared boules onto the preheated stone. Bake 35-40 minutes. Please note - baking time vary - the measure of doneness is an internal temperature of 200-210 F.
    • Let cool completely on a wire rack. (Note: not allowing the loaf to fully cool before cutting will result in a gummy texture. Let the moisture redistribute and dissapate. When completely cool, slice and serve or store in an airtight container. To re-crisp crust, toast or reheat in 375 degree oven for 5-10 minutes.

Condiments

Gravy

(Source: Authentic Canadian Poutine from Seasons and Suppers)

  • 3 Tbsp arrowroot starch
  • 2 Tbsp water
  • 6 Tbsp salted butter
  • 1/4 C almond flour
  • 2 C beef stock
  • 1 C chicken stock
  • Onion, garlic, pepper to taste

1. Prepare the gravy: in a small bowl, dissolve the starch in water and set it aside.

2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter. Add the flour, and cook, stirring regularly, for about 5 minutes, until the mixture turns golden brown.

3. Add the beef and chicken broth, and bring to a boil, stirring with a whisk. Stir in the starch and simmer for 3 - 5 minutes or until sauce thickens. Season with pepper. Taste and add additional salt, if necessary, to taste. Make ahead and re-warm or keep warm until your fries are ready.

Paleo Worcestershire

  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 3 Tbsp (33g) mustard seed
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 2 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tsp (2g) peppercorns
  • 1 Tbsp grated gingerroot
  • 1 cinnamon stick (3" stick = 1 tsp ground)
  • 1 tsp (2g) whole cloves (1 tsp whole = 0.75 tsp ground)
  • 1 - 1/2 cups (335 mL) cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup (120 mL) balsamic vinegar
  • 2 Tbsp (40g) honey
  • 1/2 cup (30g) tamarind pulp
  • 1/2 tsp curry powder
  • 2 anchovy fillets
  • 1/2 cup (120 mL) water
  • salt

In a large, nonreactive saucepan, combine everything. Put over low heat and bring to a simmer. Keep it on very low flame until onions are soft - 45 minutes to an hour. Taste it and decide if you think it needs more salt - I added about a teaspoon. YIELD: 36 servings (1 Tbsp each)

Cheese Sauce

  • 2 Tbsp coconut oil
  • 2 Tbsp arrowroot powder
  • 1 - 1/4 C full fat coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp tahini or almond butter, heaping
  • 2 Tbsp nutritional yeast, heaping
  • 1 - 1/2 Tbsp white wine (or white wine vinegar)
  • 1/4 tsp Dijon mustard
  • 1 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp
  • 1/2 tsp chicken boullion paste
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/4 tsp onion powder
  • 1/4 tsp paprika (colour)

Melt coconut oil on low heat and mix in arrowroot, whisking for 20 seconds before slowly adding the coconut milk. Whisk and blend paste well, bring to a bubbly boil, then turn heat to low.

Add tahini, nutritional yeast, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, salt, garlic, onion, white wine, and paprika. Whisk together really well. Remove from heat and set aside.
